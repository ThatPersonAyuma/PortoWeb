Who I Am


I am a technology enthusiast with a strong passion for low-level programming and cyber security. I enjoy exploring how systems work at their core — from computer architecture and operating systems to network protocols and software security. On campus, I actively engage in activities through student organizations and laboratory projects, which help me sharpen both my technical and collaborative skills.
I am always open to new opportunities and projects that align with my field of interest. Whether it’s research, system development, or contributing to open-source, I am eager to keep learning and building. My interests also extend to reverse engineering, exploit development, and automation tools, as I believe technology is not just about theory but about creating practical solutions that bring real impact.
